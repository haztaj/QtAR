Privacy Policy — Quran Recite

Last updated: 10 July 2026

Quran Recite ("the app") detects which verse (ayah) of the Qur'an is being recited, so it can follow along on the page in real time. This policy explains exactly what the app does and does not do with your data. In short: the app does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data, and all audio is processed entirely on your device.

Microphone & audio

  • The app uses your device microphone only while detection is active (after you start a session).
  • Audio is analysed in real time, entirely on your device, to recognise the verse being recited. It is never sent to any server and never leaves your device.
  • The app does not record or save your audio in normal use. Audio frames are processed and immediately discarded.
  • An optional developer debug feature — off by default and only active if you explicitly turn it on — can save a recording of a session to your device's app-private storage to help diagnose issues. Any such recording stays on your device and is shared only if you choose to share it. You can delete it at any time.

Data collection

The app has no user accounts, no advertising, no third-party analytics, and no tracking of any kind. It does not collect your name, contacts, location, device identifiers, or any other personal information.

Network use

The app connects to the internet only to download its recognition model and the Qur'an page fonts (hosted as public files on GitHub). These are ordinary file downloads — no personal data is sent in these requests beyond what any web download involves (e.g. your IP address, which the hosting provider may log). After these files are downloaded once, the app's core detection works fully offline.

Children

The app is suitable for all ages and does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.
